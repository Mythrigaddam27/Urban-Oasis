Compost Strategies for Urban Gardening

Composting is a great way to reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your plants. Whether you have a small balcony or a backyard, there are composting methods that work for every space.

Benefits of Composting

  • Reduces the amount of waste going to landfills.
  • Improves soil structure and fertility.
  • Helps retain moisture in the soil, reducing the need for frequent watering.

What You Can Compost

Here are some common items that you can add to your compost pile:

  • Vegetable and fruit scraps
  • Eggshells
  • Grass clippings
  • Leaves and small branches
  • Used coffee grounds and filters

What to Avoid Composting

  • Meat and dairy products
  • Grease, oils, and fats
  • Diseased plants
  • Pet waste
  • Inorganic materials (plastic, metals, etc.)

Composting Methods

There are several methods you can use to compost, depending on your available space:

  • Cold Composting: Learn more about Cold Composting
  • Hot Composting: Learn more about Hot Composting
  • Vermi Composting: Learn more about Vermi Composting

Maintaining Your Compost

To keep your compost healthy, make sure to:

  • Turn your compost regularly to aerate it.
  • Keep a balance of green (nitrogen-rich) and brown (carbon-rich) materials.
  • Ensure the compost stays moist but not waterlogged.
